    Payment Posting and Secondary Insurance Submission

  • In order to reduce claim denial and delayed reimbursement, we:

    • Enter all patient demographics and insurance information correctly
    • Verify CPT and ICD codes against our database, which we update frequently
    • Use appropriate modifiers
    • Obtain medical reports and submit them with the claim if necessary
    • Forward all secondary and tertiary insurance claims to secondary/tertiary insurances after a primary insurance payment is made via either electronic or paper submission

  • After rigorous scrubbing, a clean claim is generated and submitted to insurance companies either electronically using a clearing house or on paper if the company does not have an electronic payer ID. At CMM, we believe in efficiency; all claims are submitted as soon as the billing log is received.

  • Prompt posting of payments to the patient’s account:

    • Whether payment is received from the insurance, patient, or attorney, all payments are posted to the patient’s account promptly recording contractual adjustment and patient responsibility.
    • All collections from insurance companies and patients are reconciled at the end of the day to make sure no payment is left unposted

  • Persistent follow up and appeals on all unpaid claims and accounts

    Timely follow up is key for high reimbursement, and we are experts at chasing down insurance companies and getting results. Our follow up strategy includes:

    • Obtaining reports of claims submitted successfully from clearing house
    • Promptly contacting insurance companies when a claim is denied and resubmitting the claim and any necessary supporting documents after the appropriate corrections are made
    • Negotiating with attorneys, insurance companies, and third parties to resolve disputed accounts
    • Tracking all claims to ensure they are filled promptly and within the timely filing limit

  • Creation and mailing of patient statements

    • Patients without insurance receive their statement as soon as the data is entered.
    • Patients with insurance receive their statement if any balance remains after the payment from the insurance company is posted
    • Forward all secondary and tertiary insurance claims to secondary/tertiary insurances after a primary insurance payment is made via either electronic or paper submission

  • Courteous but effective collection of outstanding receivables

    • Every patient receives three monthly statements, giving them enough time to communicate with CMM and pay their balance. If the patient does not respond, their account is sent to a collection company upon the provider’s approval.
    • We offer several payment options to patients, and the payment plan can be discussed prior to being offered.

  • Answering patient billing questions professionally and efficiently

    • The provider’s office doesn’t have to spend their valuable time answering the patient’s phone calls about billing questions.
    • Our courteous and professional staff members answer all billing-related questions and help the patient understand what’s going on with their account.

  • Customized monthly statistical reports and spreadsheets

    At the beginning of each month, the following reports are sent to the provider to track business activity and productivity:

    • A monthly summary including charges, payments, contractual write offs and patient adjustments
    • A report of payments received from insurance companies and patients
    • A report of trends in payment and charges, which can help you understand the patterns of your practice and allow you to make informed changes

    At the end of the year, we send a summary of trends in your practice, giving you a clear picture of your annual performance.

    Other customized reports can be provided per the provider’s request.

  • Specialty-specific medical coding services for both diagnosis and procedure

    As an extension of your office remotely, we can Scrub the claims on your computers to ensure all claims are entered correctly, appropriate modifiers are used, and, above all, the codes are correct

  • New Practice Set-Up

    If you are considering, or in the midst of, starting your own practice, you may find yourself feeling overwhelmed by the amount of work required. A stable start provides a strong foundation and long-term financial security, but if you don’t follow the right steps, you will find yourself in a bind. We can lay out a roadmap to help you start your practice seamlessly and run uninterrupted.

    For office-based practices, CMM can help you choose your software, furniture, office and exam room supplies, logo, and stationery. It may take 3 to 6 months before you are ready to start seeing patients in your office and hospital, so make sure you act now!

  • We take the following steps to set up new practices:

    1. Identify the practice and its financial objectives
    2. Name the practice and structure as an LLC, INC, S Corporation, etc.
    3. Register the organization acquire a Tax ID
    4. Obtain a group NPI under the Tax ID
    5. Get individual NPIs for each physician and PA in the practice
    6. Credentialing with insurance companies
    7. Create a business plan and budget
    8. Hire staff and train them to run the practice smoothly
    9. Design and implement a marketing plan to help you develop a long-term relationship with your patients

  • Staff Training

    The success of a medical practice does not depend solely on the physician – it’s a team effort. Your medical staff is equally responsible for helping your practice run efficiently and seamlessly. We can provide the proper training and open communication necessary to unlock your staff’s full potential, increasing not only productivity in the office but also your cash flow. We provide training to both new hires and established staff members.

    By partnering with CMM, you will be improving quality, lowering costs, and enhancing the patient experience through a reliable and effective communication strategy.

    Our training includes:
  • Developing a Policy

    Our staff training policy is customized for your practice and its needs. We provide training to both front office and back office staff. Our policy includes continuous communication to keep all employees up to date on office policies, compliances, and job-specific requirements.

  • Ongoing Collaboration

    If your staff members don’t work as a team, the success of your medical practice may be impeded. In order to run your practice successfully and deliver quality care to your patients, it is important for your staff to undergo collaboration training. We prepare your staff to work in a collaborative manner, with a goal of forming a cohesive team that strives to produce positive outcomes and contribute to the success of your organization.

  • Assigning Specific Skills

    The most important step in team coordination is determining which team members will be responsible for specific aspects of patient care. The focus of the team should be on the needs of the patient, and patient care goals should determine team selection and delegation of responsibilities. Well trained staff members translate to a better customer service experience, which will keep your patients loyal to you and may even attract new patients to your practice.

  • Cross-Training

    Cross-training is a valuable tool that can improve staff morale and prepare your staff to handle any situation. Your staff members will feel like valuable assets to the organization if they are given the opportunity to expand their skills and knowledge. Moreover, if your staff is cross-trained, you won’t face any crises if an employee takes time off work or leaves the practice.

  • Continuous Communication

    Continuous communication is needed to facilitate coordinated care at all times. Lack of communication can lead to medical errors. It is crucial for your staff to have standardized communication tools and an environment in which individuals feel safe speaking up and expressing concerns. We can help establish an effective communication system that includes regularly scheduled meetings to discuss patient care issues, removing or retraining of an unproductive staff.
